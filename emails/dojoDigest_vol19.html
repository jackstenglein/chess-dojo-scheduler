Updates

It’s been a very busy month at the Dojo as we prepare for Dojo 4.0. The big push has been working on our beta version of our “Training Planner”. The idea has been with us for years, and was especially advocated for by dojoers who were also weightlifters. These buff dudes want a program, a regime, something that will take all the guesswork out of studying. And the Dojo agrees, a pillar of the program is that it’s a huge advantage to not have to think about what you should study. Fretting over the what and how costs not only energy but encourages putting the work off.

Big fights were had behind the scenes about what we should call it! DojoMentor, DojoCoach and the simple Weekly Training Plan have all been proposed, but nothing has stuck. What do you think we should call it? We also need your help with making it as user friendly as possible. Join the Discord here!

It’s a long-term project. One refinement that might not be too far off is to do a computer analysis of a player’s games and use that as a basis for what they should study. We discussed the germ of this idea in our podcast with statistician Kenneth Regan.

Below is an example of what the UI now looks like, using GM Kraai as an example. One simple change that effected his studying habits was the Dojo giving a check mark to any week where you hit your training goal. You can set your own hours. Kraai set 15/week and you can see that the new trainer has had a positive influence on BoomerGM.


Requirements - Another key task for Dojo 4.0 (always our May 1st birthday) is that the senseis carefully review all of the required material. We consider feedback, new courses/books and the availability of the material. But most importantly we review the profiles of those who haven’t made it yet despite having done the work. For the premise of the dojo is that if you do the work you will make it to the next cohort (at least to a high degree of probability!). Every year we do this process, always trying to make the plan better.

Dojo Tournaments now have prizes! An anonymous donor had a simple thought: we should be playing for stakes. The problem with cash stakes of course is that it brings cheaters. And the Dojo is very proud of the fact that its culture makes it the best place to play longer games and play them without cheaters. So the first place of both the round robins and Open Classical will now be entitled to a free year at the dojo and the right to play in a year-end tournament of champions. Watch the video about the DojoCircuit here.

How to Analyze Your Games – A ChessDojo Guide is finally coming out on Kindle and the ChessDojo store! Keep an eye out for it's release on ForwardChess soon.

Patreon and DojoTalks - The Dojo is trying to be more consistent with our podcast, but we need your help! Head over to our Patreon where you can get episodes early, vote on coming topics, suggest topics and discuss the pod on our private discord.
