Training Program Updates

Dojo Game Editor & Viewer - The main new features we've added are listed below and also covered in this video.

  • Free Tier - Free-tier users can now use our game editor to analyze their games, although their games must be unlisted (see below).
  • OTB Games - You can now create a blank game at the starting position and manually enter your moves - perfect for OTB games.
  • Unlisted Games - You can mark your game as unlisted while working on your annotations. You can still share the game with the URL, but otherwise it will not be viewable to anyone else. Combined with the above feature, this allows you to do all your analysis on the site, whether for OTB or online games.
  • Clock Times - For years sensei Kraai had been looking for a way to easily enter the clock times of every move into his game annotations. It's such an important piece of information! He even wrote Chessbase about it, but they did nothing. Our editor now allows you to easily enter the clock times and also displays a visual graph of both players' times. It's an amazing tool. At a glance you can now see a key storyline of any game. In the first graduate show with the new feature, Kraai saw more clearly what he had only sniffed before: many Dojoers are not spending their time. Slow down! So in addition to being a really cool feature, we are hoping that this new graph helps players visualize how many points they are leaving on the table when they move too quickly.
  • Autosave - The game editor automatically saves your work as you go, so that you never lose your analysis.
  • Position Database - The position database is now available when viewing or editing a game. Search through the Dojo's database or through the Lichess database.
  • No Engine - Whereas Chess.com and Lichess bombard the user with computer analysis as their default setting, the Dojo game editor has no engine at all! The Dojo does it right.

Women's Channel - The Dojo and the chess world in general can be an intimidating place for women. It often feels like it's just dudes. But there are women in the Dojo and they now have their own private Discord channel. Send a Discord message to DMHokie to request access.
