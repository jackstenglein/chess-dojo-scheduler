Updates

August was a month in which many long-term dojo projects continued to be built out behind the scenes: 1) The senseis continue to build out the tests and at least half the struggle is the tech side of the issue, in particular how we can effectively rate problems. 2) We’ve been working on the updated Dojo rating translator and hope to have that ready some time in September. 3) After rolling out the dojo masters database last month Jack is now close to a new folders system where members can place their own pgns, such as opening files. 4) We then hope to have our own openings trainer that dojoers can then input those opening pgns and then train them (November?). 5) You will also have noticed many small changes in the Dojo user interface. This is part of long-term attempt to make our site more user-friendly and fun. 6) Badges are on their way for specific achievements! 7) The Dojo book How to Analyze your Games continues to be edited and will hopefully be out soon. 8) Sensei Kraai is almost done with a Chessable course called Time to do What featuring forty training positions from Dojo games in the 1000-1500 range, covering the most common opportunity at this level: your opponent has given you time, and you have to figure out what to do. Das Tempo ist die Seele des Schaches (Time is the Soul of Chess) – Siegbert Tarrasch..

Dojo Arenas - Way back at the beginning of the Dojo, the senseis hosted a Twitch show called Friday Night Fights. The first thing David did when he returned from rainy France was to reinstitute this tradition in a much bigger way. He wants to do commentary on your games! Friday Night Fights begins at 11pm ET/5am CET and the Dojo Rapid begins at 12 ET/6pm CET every Sunday. Commentary on the events can be found, live, at Twitch.tv/ChessDojo as well as on youtube.com/@ChessDojo.

Teams - Are you part of a scholastic or European club team? Let us know! We’d like to bring your teammates to the dojo and see you with them on a customized scoreboard. We believe the dojo is the place for teams to track their progress, be competitive with one another and challenge other teams.
