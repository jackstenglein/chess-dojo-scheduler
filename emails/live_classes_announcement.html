This January (2026), the Dojo will start teaching live group lessons. The classes will be available as part of a monthly membership tier which also includes access to the rest of the Dojo Training Program and website. We're interested in your feedback! Take our survey or join the waitlist here.

With the exception of the Game & Profile Review classes, each lesson will be open to all members, but students should note that the instruction will be aimed at the level described.

 

Additional notes:

  • Each class will be interactive, with students encouraged to participate and ask questions.
  • Every series of classes will come with a corresponding discord channel / message feed for students to use.
  • Students will be facilitated in forming peer review study groups to meet and work on material outside of classes.
  • All classes will be recorded and available for viewing on-demand.

Class Descriptions

 

Game & Profile Review

0-600, 600-1000, 1000-1400, 1400-1800, 1800+ depending on interest

All senseis

 

A weekly one-hour deep dive into one of the students' progress. Every week a different student will get the spotlight as the coach reviews their training profile along with 1 or more of their annotated games. Students will be encouraged to meet as a group prior to the class and review the game beforehand. GM Jesse Kraai has piloted this class and the peer review group improved annotations, prepared students to reflect deeply on a given game and created a great group camaraderie that carried over from month to month.

 

 

Endgame Fundamentals

0-1200

GM Jesse Kraai

 

A class focusing on key endgame algorithms and decision-making. GM Jesse will go through some of the most important endgames to know, as well as talk about how to approach the endgame in general. Don't skip endgame day!

 

 

Building an Opening Repertoire

1200-1800

IM Kostya Kavutskiy

 

A class on building and maintaining an opening repertoire. IM Kostya will discuss his process of working on openings, how to use various tools, work with engines, and answer all questions about how the sausage is made.

 

 

The Najdorf

1400-1800

IM David Pruess

 

IM David will be teaching the Sicilian Najdorf from scratch, one of the richest openings in chess. The class will focus on the key ideas and themes of the opening, along with typical middlegame and endgame concepts. If there is interest, David will do a class for 1800+ students as well.

 

 

Calculation Training

Level TBD based on sign-ups

IM Kostya Kavutskiy

 

A weekly class on calculation, focusing on various techniques and skills within calculation. Students will be given weekly homework to work on before the next class, and encouraged to form study groups to solve the material together.
