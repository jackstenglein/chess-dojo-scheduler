Message from the Senseis

The senseis have noticed that most players are not consistent in their training. On the scoreboard you can see a player's work in the last seven days and last thirty days. Consistency is the only way to achieve long-term results.

Tip of the Month

The baseline for a Dojo Point on the scoreboard is playing one classical game. That takes about 2-4 hours depending on the cohort. And in general, it takes a similar amount of time to achieve a point via other categories.

This last month has seen about 12 graduations per week! In the weekly graduation shows, it becomes very obvious that the people who are earning the most Dojo Points are also the ones graduating. Time to start checking off boxes!

Site Updates

If you haven't been on the site in a while, you're missing out on these updates:

  • Free Tier - We now offer a completely free version of the program to get an overall feel for the site. You get access to a few tasks per category, the activity and time tracker, the ability to book calendar events and more. Sign-up for free at dojoscoreboard.com.
  • Weekly Tournaments & Sparring - The Dojo is now running blitz, rapid, classical and sparring tournaments every week through our Lichess club. View the full schedule here. Get your sparring done!
  • Rating graphs - Your profile page now shows your rating history since joining the Dojo.
  • Daily & Weekly Activity Tracker - On the scoreboard you can now see your activity for the last week, month and 90 days.
  • User Search On the Scoreboard - Trying to find someone on the scoreboard but you don't know their cohort? Use the new search page.
  • Game Comments - You can now post a comment or question to any game in the Dojo Database. If someone comments on a game you posted, you'll get a notification.
  • Profile Pic - You can now upload a pic of yourself or your favorite avatar.
