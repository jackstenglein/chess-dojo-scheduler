Training Program Updates

The Dojo has been busy! The last couple months have seen a variety of improvements to the site and the Dojo. We're proud of this growth and hope it continues!

Scorebook - We've released the ChessDojo Scorebook! It's a great way to keep all your OTB games in one place for later review. DMHokie led the charge on this one, and it's our first stab at publishing. Our next big project will be a book called "How To Analyze Your Games" which we are hoping to have out at the beginning of next year. For those just starting out OTB, view all our tournament gear recommendations here.

Game Explorer - We've added an opening explorer to the site! This allows Dojoers to search for a position in both the Dojo database and the Lichess database. You can also subscribe to a position to get notified when a new game is added to the Dojo database containing that position.

Full Dojo Scoreboard - You can now compare your achievements across the entire dojo! Who has gained the most Elo? Who has studied the longest? Who has the highest Dojo Score? From the scoreboard dropdown menu look for the "Full Dojo" option.

Scoreboard for People You Follow - Also in the scoreboard dropdown menu, you can find "People I Follow." There you can see all the vitals of the Dojoers you are most interested in.

Dojo Courses - For our free tier members, you can now buy our opening courses and access them without needing a training plan subscription! And soon we will be able to offer courses by both senseis and others on a wide range of topics. Imagine King's Indian camp w/ Kostya, Calculation w/ GM Friedel, the possibilities are endless. We hope to list some of these new course offerings in the next Dojo Digest.

Australian Rating System - We have several players from Down Under. They can now user their home federation (ACF) as their preferred Elo and the scoreboard will automatically track it.

Achievements

Using the new Full Dojo Scoreboard, we can now see who has gained the most Elo. Top honor belongs to Covbob who has gained an astonishing 700 points since joining the Dojo. He's working hard on his chess too - 137 Dojo points and 110 hours of work. Go Bob! Top Dojo Score goes to Gikabyte. Gika's work shows up in three graduations! And the most time spent goes to the famous NoseKnowsAll with 1361 hours!


Kostya had a nice tournament at the 2023 U.S. Masters with 6.0/9, tying for second place u2450. While far from a GM norm, it will help his push to the necessary 2500 Elo mark.


Chris Coski, aka Chris-C had a breakthrough tournament at the West Virginia Senior and has gone above the difficult 1800 USCF barrier! Gaining over 200 points in a year, Chris proves that old guys can still improve!

Truxcore (rated 1207) finished 2nd in an u1600 section with 4.5/5, won some $$ and went up 239 rating points for an all-time new high!

MrPhyr won the U1500 section of the Nassau Fall Swiss, scoring 4/4 and earning a new USCF peak rating of 1342.

Admonishment

Too many Dojoers are in the wrong cohort! This is not only bad for the player, but also for the cohort. When you reach the rating for the next cohort, graduate! You don't need to complete all the tasks in your current cohort first. And if you drop down below your current cohort, switch cohorts. We will soon have a popup that pesters those who need to promote or demote themselves.
